The Incredible Story Of Thin Lizzy

Pre-History (Early 1960's)

  • Two of the founding members of Thin Lizzy, Phil Lynott and Brian Downey first met at school in Dublin, Ireland.
  • Lynott joined a local band called 'The Black Eagles', as vocalist in 1963, and Downey was recruited as their drummer in 1965
  • In 1967, Lynott was asked to join another band called 'Skid Row' by guitarist Brush Shiels,who also brought Belfast guitarist Gary Moore into the band 1968.
  • After a disappointing television appearance in June 1969, Shiels fired Lynott, he however subsequently taught Lynott to play bass guitar.
  • Lynott then reunited with Downey and formed another band 'Orphanage' with Lynott as vocalist & guitarist whilst Downey was the bands drummer

Early Years (1969–1972)

  • In December 1969 a guitarist named Eric Bell and organist named Eric Wrixon met by chance in a pub where Orphanage was performing.
  • Eric Bell introduced himself during a break and they agreed to form a band using Lynotts compositions, thus making the initial line up a quartet.
  • In July 1970, the band released a single, "The Farmer"/"I Need You",it only sold 283 copies and Wrixon left the band before the single was released.
  • In the end of 1970 Thin Lizzy signed to Decca Records and travelled to London in January 1971 to record their debut album titled 'Thin Lizzy'
  • The album sold moderately well but did not chart in the U.K despite airplay and support from influential DJs John Peel and Kid Jensen
  • Despite poor sales of their debut album, Decca agreed to finance the band's second album titled 'Shades of a Blue Orphanage',it was released in March 1972
  • In late 1972, Decca released Thin Lizzy's version of a traditional Irish ballad, "Whiskey in the Jar", as a single.It topped the Irish chart, and reached no. 6 in the U.K

Mid-Life Years (1973-1975)

  • The band's next album, Vagabonds of the Western World, was released in September 1973 following strong airplay in the U.K, but again failed to chart.
  • Eric Bell suddenly left the band on New Year's Eve 1973 in the middle of a tour and young ex-Skid Row guitarist Gary Moore was recruited to continue with tour
  • Moore stayed until April 1974 & Lynott expanded the line-up with two guitarists instead of one,in order to complete a scheduled tour of Germany in May 1974
  • After the initial unsuccessful recruits Lynott and Downey eventually settled on two guitarists Brian Robertson and Scott Gorham
  • In late 1974 the new line-up secured a record deal with Phonogram, and released their forth album titled Nightlife.It was considered a dissapointment & failed to chart

The Golden Years (1975-1979)

  • In early 1975,Thin Lizzy toured the United States for the first time, in support of Bob Seger and Bachman–Turner Overdrive (BTO)
  • Thin Lizzy recorded their fifth album titled 'Fighting', which became the first Thin Lizzy album to chart in the U.K, reaching no. 60
  • In 1976 the band recorded the album 'Jailbreak', which proved to be their life changing and breakthrough record after the first five albums failed to chart.
  • Jailbreak featured the hit song "The Boys Are Back in Town" which reached no.8 in the U.K, and no.12 in the US.It is their most successful single to date
  • In August 1976 Thin Lizzy recorded 'Johnny The Fox',and had a successful tour and television appearances to support the alum across the U.K and U.S
  • Thin Lizzy flew to Canada in May 1977 to record their seventh album titled 'Bad Reputation', it sold very well, reaching no. 4 in the U.K
  • In 1978, Thin Lizzy released their first live album titled 'Live and Dangerous'.It reached no. 2 in the U.K and was a great success
  • In early 1979 they recorded 'Black Rose: A Rock Legend in Paris',the album reached no. 2 in the U.K despite the increasing drug habits of Lynott and Gorham

Later Years And Break Up (1980–1983)

  • In April 1980,Thin Lizzy released the album titled 'Chinatown',it reached no. 7 in the U.K but this time failed to reach the top 100 in the U.S
  • Lynott began to work a lot with other bands to try and remain relevant and released a solo album titled 'Solo in Soho'
  • In April 1981, the band's first greatest hits album titled 'The Adventures of Thin Lizzy' was released, and reached no. 6 in the U.K to moderate success
  • Lynott's second solo album titled 'The Philip Lynott Album', was delayed until 1982 while the single 'Renegade' was completed and released in November 1981
  • The last Thin Lizzy album titled 'Thunder and Lightning' was released in March 1983,and was much more successful than its predecessor, reaching no.4 in the U.K
  • Thin Lizzy played their final UK concert before their break-up at the Reading Festival on 28 August 1983
